Junior Research Fellow – Continuum Dynamics & Control Lab

As a JRF at the CDC Lab, I work on trajectory control of one-dimensional flexible structures, with a focus on Euler–Bernoulli beams.

  • Develop FEM models of beams using Hermite shape functions and validate natural frequencies against analytical estimates.
  • Build Simscape Multibody models and compare them with Simulink implementations to study discretisation effects on phase and amplitude.
  • Design a laser-based displacement measurement system with LabVIEW, NI hardware, current-to-voltage converters, and Chebyshev filters.
  • Use FFT-based techniques to estimate mode shapes and damping from experimental data.

The long-term goal is closed-loop vibration control using distributed sensing and actuation.
